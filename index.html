20 sale slots per person shown — scroll right to see them all.
How to use: Click any Year / Model / Stock box and type directly — it saves as you type. Press Enter to jump straight to the next field. Each car also has its own Type tag and Note box right below it, so those always stay with that car. Pick a highlight color above, then click a box (or click-drag isn't needed — just click each box in the entry) to color-code it. Click "No fill" then a box to clear its color. Search: use the search box in the toolbar to find a car by stock number or model — matching boxes get a blue outline and the board scrolls to the first match. Sold / Pending: once a car's Year and Model are both filled in, it's counted automatically — a Stock # isn't required. Check the small box next to that car's Note to auto-fill the word "pending" into the note (so you don't have to type it) and move the car to the Pending total — uncheck it to remove the word and move it back to Sold. You can still just type "pending" yourself too — the checkbox stays in sync either way, and any other note text you've added stays put. Clear the Year and Model and the car drops out of both totals. New / Used: click the small tag under a car's Year/Model/Stock to mark it — click cycles blank → NEW → USED → blank, and the New/Used totals in the header update automatically. Deleting a salesperson: check the small box in the top-left corner of their name (their row gets a light red tint), then click "Delete Salesperson" at the bottom and confirm. Undo: clearing all data or deleting a salesperson both show a brief "Undo" option at the bottom of the screen right after. Month: the dropdown in the header remembers your last selection automatically, and is also saved into the file when you save. To keep your edits: click the 💾 Save Board button any time — or just press Ctrl/Cmd + S. In Chrome or Edge, this asks where you'd like to save it the first time, then keeps saving straight to that same file after that (open the page again later and it'll ask again). In Firefox or Safari it downloads straight to your default downloads folder instead. A small "Last saved" note appears next to the button after each save. If you try to close the page with unsaved changes, your browser will ask you to confirm first. Sorting: the board automatically re-sorts so whoever has the most cars out (filled or color-coded boxes) sits at the top of the list.
